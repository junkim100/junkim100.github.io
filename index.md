By making these systems more transparent and predictable, I strive to enhance their reliability, safety, and alignment with human values.
      </p>

Through rigorous research and collaboration, I aim to help build a future where AI technologies are both transformative and responsible.
      </p>

      <p>

If you share my passion for advancing AI responsibly or have ideas in interpretability or safety, feel free to reach out—I am always eager to collaborate with like-minded researchers.
      </p>

    <div class="research-interests mt-5">
      <h3 class="mb-4">Research Interests</h3>
      <div class="interests-grid">
        <div class="interest-item">
          <h4>Mechanistic Interpretability</h4>
          <p>
            My primary focus is on reverse-engineering LLMs to uncover their internal circuits, algorithms, and decision-making processes. This includes:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Analyzing transformer architectures to map dependencies between attention heads, layers, and emergent behaviors</li>
            <li>Developing sparse autoencoders as tools for isolating interpretable features in high-dimensional latent spaces</li>
            <li>Investigating causal relationships between model components and specific capabilities through probing techniques</li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            By understanding these mechanisms, I aim to make LLMs more transparent while providing insights into their strengths and limitations.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="interest-item">
          <h4>AI Safety</h4>
          <p>
            Ensuring the safe deployment of advanced AI systems is critical to my work. My research in AI safety focuses on:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Developing scalable frameworks for aligning model behavior with human values through fine-tuning and reinforcement learning</li>
            <li>Detecting and mitigating biases in language models using interpretability-driven methods</li>
            <li>Building robust anomaly detection systems that identify harmful or unexpected behaviors during deployment</li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            My goal is to create methodologies that safeguard against risks while enabling reliable and ethical applications of AI.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="interest-item">
          <h4>Mechanistic Anomaly Detection</h4>
          <p>
            Mechanistic anomaly detection involves identifying unexpected or harmful behaviors in LLMs by analyzing their internal mechanisms. I focus on:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Developing tools that trace causal pathways within neural networks to diagnose sources of anomalous outputs</li>
            <li>Designing self-monitoring models capable of detecting deviations from intended behavior during real-world use</li>
            <li>Applying interpretability techniques to improve system robustness under adversarial or high-stakes conditions</li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            This research ensures that AI systems remain reliable even when faced with complex or unpredictable environments.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="interest-item">
          <h4>Reasoning Models & Agent Systems</h4>
          <p>
            Understanding how LLMs reason and interact as agents is an emerging focus of my work. This includes:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Investigating multi-step reasoning processes within transformer-based architectures</li>
            <li>Developing agent systems that leverage LLMs for planning, decision-making, and interactive problem-solving tasks</li>
            <li>Exploring how compositionality in neural networks enables structured reasoning across diverse domains</li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            By advancing reasoning models, I aim to enable LLMs to perform complex tasks reliably while maintaining interpretability.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="interest-item">
          <h4>Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)</h4>
          <p>
            RAG combines retrieval systems with generative models to enhance factual accuracy and groundedness. My work in this area focuses on:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Designing retrieval pipelines optimized for domain-specific knowledge integration</li>
            <li>Exploring hybrid architectures that improve factual consistency in generative outputs</li>
            <li>Reducing hallucinations by embedding retrieval mechanisms directly into transformer workflows</li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            Through RAG techniques, I aim to bridge the gap between generative capabilities and real-world reliability.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="interest-item">
          <h4>Computational Neuroscience</h4>
          <p>
            Computational neuroscience bridges the gap between biological neural systems and artificial intelligence, offering insights into how natural intelligence can inspire better AI systems. My work in this area focuses on:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Studying biological neural mechanisms to inform the development of more efficient and interpretable AI architectures</li>
            <li>Drawing parallels between attention mechanisms in transformers and cognitive processes observed in the brain</li>
            <li>Exploring how memory, reasoning, and learning in biological systems can be modeled computationally to improve LLM performance</li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            By leveraging principles from neuroscience, I aim to enhance our understanding of both natural and artificial intelligence, driving innovations in AI research.
          </p>
        </div>

      </div>

    <h2 class="mb-4">Education and Research Journey</h2>
    <p class="journey-intro mb-5">
      My academic path reflects an evolving fascination with complex systems—from spatial computing to neural architectures—driven by the fundamental question: "How do intelligent systems truly work?" This journey has crystallized into a focused mission to reverse-engineer AI while ensuring its safe and ethical development.
    </p>

    <div class="timeline">
      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-date">2024 – Present</div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
          <h4>Korea University, Seoul, South Korea</h4>
          <p>
            As a Master's researcher in the <a href="https://nlp.korea.ac.kr/">NLP&AI Lab</a> under <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=HMTkz7oAAAAJ&hl=en">Dr. Heui-Seok Lim</a>, I have been involved in multiple government and industry-funded projects, including collaborations with the <a href="https://www.mfds.go.kr/index.do">Ministry of Food and Drug Safety</a> and <a href="https://corp.kt.com/eng/">KT Gen AI Lab</a>. My work focuses on advancing LLM interpretability, AI safety, and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems. Key contributions include:
          </p>
          <ul>
            <li>Developing a novel knowledge editing method for domain-specific applications, enabling precise updates to LLMs without retraining</li>
            <li>Designing automatic attack detection frameworks (harmfulness/bias detection) for AI safety through automated red-teaming techniques</li>
            <li>Creating an advanced RAG agent system capable of dynamic knowledge retrieval and integration for real-time decision-making tasks</li>
          </ul>
          <p>
            These projects integrate cutting-edge interpretability techniques with practical applications, bridging theoretical AI research with real-world deployment challenges. Recent breakthroughs include a new probing method to trace causal pathways in transformer architectures, currently under review at ACL 2025.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="timeline-item">
        <div class="timeline-date">2019 – 2023</div>
        <div class="timeline-content">
