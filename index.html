About Me

I'm a researcher specializing in Large Language Models (LLMs) with a specific focus on interpretability. My work aims to enhance AI systems' transparency and understandability, emphasizing the ethical implications of AI applications.

Pursuing my Ph.D. at Korea University, I explore innovative approaches to improve LLM interpretability, making AI systems more transparent and actionable in real-world settings.

Research Focus

LLM Interpretability

Focusing on improving transparency and explainability in language models, developing methods for better understanding of AI decision-making processes.

LLM Architectures

Researching and designing advanced architectures for large language models to enhance their performance and efficiency.

Sparse Autoencoders & LLM Circuits

Developing sparse autoencoders and exploring the circuitry within LLMs to optimize their interpretability and functionality.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

Integrating RAG techniques to enhance the information retrieval capabilities of language models, improving their accuracy and relevance.

Bias & Harm Mitigation

Analyzing and mitigating biases in LLMs, developing strategies to prevent harmful outputs and safeguard against jailbreak attacks.

LLM Alignment

Ensuring that large language models align with human values and intended use-cases, developing techniques to guide model behavior towards desired outcomes.
